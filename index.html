What We Do

Through mentorship, students will receive help with:

Writing Essays
Completing Applications
Obtaining Recommendations
Navigating Financial Aid
Understanding Collegiate Life

Paint the Picture

The college mentors can provide students a better idea of collegiate life than anyone else. We've been in their shoes only a few years ago and know what information they need in order to succeed in college.

Help with Essays

We can provide quality assistance with the crafting an essay to fit all kinds of applications. Our expertise in this area is broad and we are knowledgeable about the stories admissions officers are looking for. The course will prepare the students for creative thinking and meticulous writing.

Grow Their Voice

Each student will have the opportunity to receive personalized help. We know that everyone has different backgrounds and experiences, and we want the colleges to get to know them for who they are.

Get Them Ready

College is in many ways the greatest learning experience. Students will have chances to become more worldly, educated contributors to society. These students are the next world leaders. We know they will do amazing things.

Package Their Talents

Whether it's the Common App or a state school application, we want to package their talents in the most appealing way possible.

Build Their Confidence

We want to build students' confidence that they will do great, whether it is in the college applications or life beyond high school.

What Sets Us Apart

When I began the college application process junior year, I was extremely overwhelmed and felt that school didn't prepare me enough to write the essays and complete the applications. Since all of the mentors just went through this process, they know best how to help students going through it now!

Angela Moon Junior, Duke University

We will be able to bring a fresh, new perspective to incoming college students, since we were in their shoes only a couple of years ago.

Lyssa Urrutia Junior, Santa Clara University

I think that as current college students, we are most aware of what is expected of us as students and what catches the admissions office’s attention. Current high school students can attain really useful tips from us and I strive to give everyone the advice they may need to hear.

Sarah Cho Senior, University of Washington
